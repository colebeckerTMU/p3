Consumer Pricing Index - Shoppers Drug Mart

In the Consumer Pricing Index project at Shoppers Drug Mart, I spearheaded the development of a comprehensive solution aimed at accurately averaging the consumer price of medicine in various locations. This initiative was pivotal for upper management, providing them with a nuanced understanding of pricing dynamics within the geographical and pharmaceutical domain. Leveraging advanced statistical methodologies, we crafted a robust model capable of capturing pricing variations across different categories of medication at the different pharmacies across Canada. One of the key features of our solution was its flexibility, allowing upper management to access and interact with the model at varying levels of granularity. This granular view empowered decision-makers to delve deeper into specific product segments or zoom out to analyze broader pricing trends, enabling them to make informed strategic decisions with confidence. Through collaborative efforts and meticulous data analysis, we were able to streamline the process of price evaluation and optimization, ensuring competitive pricing strategies aligned with market dynamics and customer demands.

Skills Developed: Python, SQL, Microsoft Excel, Problem Solving, Big Data Analytics, Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

Machine Learning Development - TMU

In the realm of Machine Learning Development, I was tasked with creating neural network designed for the identification of cancer cells. Given sample data I embarked on the task of building a robust predictive model. Drawing upon state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms and techniques, I meticulously curated and preprocessed vast amounts of multidimensional data, including genetic markers and cellular attributes. Through iterative experimentation and rigorous model refinement, we achieved remarkable accuracy in distinguishing malignant cells from benign ones, laying the groundwork for early detection and intervention in cancer patients. Leveraging the power of deep learning, my model was able to distinguish the two types of cells using five inputs and three hidden layers. Each layer consisted of fifteen nodes.

Skills Developed: Python, PyTorch, Machine Learning, Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
